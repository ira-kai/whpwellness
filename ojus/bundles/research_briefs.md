**Individual total: $116.98**

### ⚠️ REMOVED: standalone zinc (54mg)
**Original plan included zinc standalone but combined zinc would be 79mg/day — well above 40mg UL. Risk of copper depletion and paradoxical immune suppression (Prasad et al., 2008). Removed for safety.**

### Research rationale
- Vitamin C ≥200mg/day reduced cold duration 8% (Hemila & Chalker, 2013, *Cochrane*)
- Zinc ≥75mg lozenges reduced cold duration 33% (same Cochrane review) — 25mg in immune product is maintenance dose
- Vitamin D3 reduced acute respiratory infections (Martineau et al., 2017, *BMJ*, meta-analysis n=11,321)
- NAC 600mg BID reduced symptomatic influenza (De Flora et al., 1997, *European Respiratory Journal*)
- Medicinal mushroom beta-glucans activate macrophages/dendritic cells via Dectin-1 receptor (Guggenheim et al., 2014)
- Turkey Tail PSK/PSP has strongest mushroom evidence, including adjunctive oncology use in Japan

### Dosage notes
- Combined Vitamin C: 1000mg (600+400). Within recommended 200-2000mg range.
- Quercetin at 250mg is below most RCT doses (500-1000mg). Honest positioning: supportive dose, not therapeutic.
- NAC 600mg matches study doses.

### Safety: Vitamin C >1g/day increases oxalate — caution with kidney stone history.

### Research rationale — maps to liver detox phases:
**Phase I (oxidation):** R-Lipoic Acid, Turmeric (CYP450 modulation)
**Phase II (conjugation):** NAC → Glutathione conjugation; DIM for estrogen glucuronidation/sulfation; Silymarin upregulates Phase II enzymes
**Phase III (elimination):** Dandelion + Artichoke increase bile flow for conjugate excretion

Key citations:

### Dosage notes
- NAC at 500mg is slightly below the typical 600mg study dose. Still therapeutically relevant.
- This is the **strongest mechanistic bundle** — textbook integrative liver protocol.

**Bundle price (12% off): ~$147**

### ⚠️ MANDATORY DRUG INTERACTION WARNING
Berberine at 1g/day inhibits CYP2D6, CYP3A4, CYP2C9. Documented interactions with:
- **Metformin** — additive hypoglycemia risk
- **Statins** — increased statin levels (CYP3A4 inhibition)
- **Warfarin** — altered anticoagulation (CYP2C9)
- **Sulfonylureas/insulin** — hypoglycemia risk

Target demographic (metabolic syndrome) is LIKELY on these medications. Warning is non-negotiable.

### Research rationale
- **Berberine 1g/day matches RCT dose exactly** — reduced HbA1c by 0.9%, comparable to metformin 1.5g/day (Yin et al., 2008, *Metabolism*). Meta-analysis of 14 RCTs confirmed (Dong et al., 2012, *Planta Medica*). Strongest single-ingredient evidence in the catalog.
- Myo + D-Chiro Inositol 40:1 ratio reflects physiological plasma ratios, superior to MI alone in PCOS (Unfer et al., 2017, *Gynecological Endocrinology*). Cochrane review confirmed (Pundir et al., 2018).
- Chromium picolinate improved insulin sensitivity (Cefalu et al., 2010, *Diabetes Care*)
- Resistant starch 15-30g/day improved insulin sensitivity by 33% (Robertson et al., 2005, *Diabetologia*)
- Berberine + ALA both activate AMPK through different mechanisms — complementary pathways

### Dosage notes
- Berberine 1g: EXACT match to positive RCTs. Strongest dosing in the lineup.
- ALA 200mg: 1/3 of the 600mg used in most studies (Ziegler et al., 2006, *Diabetes Care*). Position honestly — supportive dose, not standalone therapeutic.
- Verify inositol blend delivers ~4000mg MI + ~100mg DCI (standard clinical dose)

### Safety: HIGH — drug interactions (see above). Berberine GI side effects common. Recommend starting at 500mg and titrating. Stagger fiber introduction.
